My Story

I Was That Athlete.

Talented but inconsistent. Good meets and bad meets — never knowing which version of myself would show up. I had the skills. I just couldn't prove it when it mattered most.

That changed when I started treating my mental game as seriously as my physical training.

The breaking point

Missed Nationals. Watched from Home.

I had the routines. I had the skills. I watched guys compete on TV knowing I could match them — but I couldn't show it on the floor. Something had to change, and it wasn't more physical training.

The discovery

Learning to Train My Mind Like a Muscle

I worked with a sports therapist and dove into books — The Mind of an Athlete, The Inner Game of Tennis, The Confident Mind. Treating mental training as a daily practice changed everything. My competition hit rate went from 20% to 90%, earning me a spot at the University of Michigan.

The crucible

Every Routine Mattered for the Whole Team

College amplified every pressure. One mistake affected your lineup spot. One bad routine could swing a team score. I leaned into everything I'd learned — working with Michigan's sports psychologists, building reset routines, training to refocus under real pressure. It worked. Walk-on to competing in the biggest moments, for a team that won multiple conference titles and a national championship.

The lesson

It Wasn't Talent That Separated the Greats.

I trained alongside athletes of every level. The one thing I consistently saw in those who reached their potential wasn't raw ability — it was mindset. Most athletes never think to train it. That's exactly why I do what I do.
